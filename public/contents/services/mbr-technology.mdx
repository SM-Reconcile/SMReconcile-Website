# MBR Technology (Membrane Bioreactor)

**Membrane Bioreactor (MBR)** technology is a state-of-the-art wastewater treatment process that combines biological treatment (activated sludge) with membrane filtration. This combination eliminates the need for a secondary clarifier, resulting in a much smaller footprint and superior effluent quality.

## How It Works

In an MBR system, microorganisms break down organic matter in an aeration tank. Instead of relying on gravity settling to separate the treated water from the biomass, the mixture is pumped through a semi-permeable membrane. The membrane acts as a physical barrier, allowing only clean water to pass through while retaining all solids, bacteria, and viruses within the bioreactor.

## Key Advantages

*   **Superior Water Quality**: The ultrafiltration membranes produce crystal-clear, high-quality effluent that is often suitable for direct reuse in non-potable applications (gardening, flushing, cooling towers).
*   **Compact Footprint**: Eliminating the secondary clarifier reduces the plant's physical size by up to 50% compared to conventional systems.
*   **High Efficiency**: Operates at higher biomass concentrations (MLSS), allowing it to handle higher organic loads.
*   **Reduced Sludge**: Operates at long sludge retention times (SRT), leading to lower sludge production.

## Applications

*   Water reuse and recycling in commercial buildings and industries.
*   Retrofitting existing sewage treatment plants to increase capacity.
*   Areas with strict discharge standards or limited space.
