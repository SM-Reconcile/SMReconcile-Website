# MBBR Technology (Moving Bed Biofilm Reactor)

**Moving Bed Biofilm Reactor (MBBR)** is a highly effective biological wastewater treatment process that combines the benefits of suspended growth and attached growth processes. It is widely used for the treatment of municipal and industrial wastewater due to its flexibility, compactness, and efficiency.

## How It Works

The MBBR process utilizes specialized plastic carriers (media) that provide a large surface area for biofilm growth. These carriers are suspended in the reactor and kept in continuous motion by aeration (in aerobic systems) or mechanical mixing (in anoxic/anaerobic systems). The bacteria growing on the carriers break down organic matter and remove pollutants from the wastewater.

## Key Advantages

*   **Compact Design**: MBBR systems require a smaller footprint compared to conventional activated sludge systems.
*   **High Capacity**: The large surface area of the carriers allows for a high concentration of biomass, enabling the treatment of high-load wastewater.
*   **Stability**: The biofilm is robust and resistant to shock loads and toxic spikes.
*   **Minimal Maintenance**: The system is self-regulating and requires less operational intervention than some other technologies.
